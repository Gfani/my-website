Worst Poems

I don’t believe in worst poems. Every poem is a journey — some are rough drafts of greatness.

A Rough One

The moon was bright but then it hid,  
Behind the cloud, it slid and slid.  
I thought I’d write a perfect rhyme,  
But lost the flow and ran out of time.

Broken Meter

Roses are red, violets are... dull,  
I tried to be deep, but this line’s just null.  
The rhythm's off, I’m not quite sure,  
Why this poem feels so impure.

Sometimes the worst poems are the ones we learn the most from.
